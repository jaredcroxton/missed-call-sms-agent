04 · What you need to build this

Four components.
Then it runs itself.

Each one takes around an hour or more, sometimes longer if you hit a webhook or carrier issue. Plan for a full day end to end, plus testing.

01
Twilio Account · Your SMS Engine
1 to 1.5 hours

The trigger that detects every missed call and starts the sequence.

  1. Go to twilio.com and create a free account.
  2. Purchase a Twilio phone number. This becomes your business number or sits in front of your existing one. Around $1.50 USD per month.
  3. Open your Twilio number settings and set the call status callback URL to a Make webhook URL. You create the webhook in the next module.
  4. When a call comes in and is not answered, Twilio fires that webhook automatically with the caller's number and call status.
02
Make Scenario · Your Automation Brain
1.5 to 2 hours

The logic that turns a missed call into a perfectly-timed SMS.

  1. Go to make.com and create a free account.
  2. Create a new scenario and add a Webhooks module as the trigger.
  3. Make gives you a unique URL. Paste this into your Twilio call status callback field from module 01.
  4. Add a Filter after the webhook so the scenario only continues if the call status equals no-answer or busy. This stops it firing on answered calls.
  5. Add a Twilio module set to Send SMS.
  6. In the To field, map the caller's phone number from the webhook data. In the From field, enter your Twilio number.
  7. Write your SMS, under 160 characters.Hi, sorry we missed your call at [Business Name]. Book a time with us here: [Calendly link]
  8. Turn the scenario on.
03
Calendly Link · Your Booking System
30 to 45 min

The customer taps once, books, and you stop playing phone tag forever.

  1. Go to calendly.com and create a free account if you do not have one.
  2. Set up a meeting type that suits your business. A 15-minute enquiry call works for most.
  3. Copy your Calendly link and paste it into the SMS message body in Make.
  4. The customer taps the link and books directly. No phone tag. No back and forth.
04
Airtable Log · Your Record Of Everything
45 min to 1 hour

A live ledger of every missed call and what happened next.

  1. Go to airtable.com and create a free account.
  2. Create a new base called Missed Calls.
  3. Add these columns: Caller Number, Date and Time, SMS Sent (yes/no), Reply Received (yes/no), Booked (yes/no), Notes.
  4. Back in Make, add an Airtable module after the SMS module and set it to create a new record every time an SMS fires.
  5. Map the caller number and timestamp from the Twilio webhook data into the correct Airtable fields.
  6. Every missed call is now logged automatically with no manual input.

Total build time, first-timer: 5 to 7 hours of focused work, often spread across two days once testing and carrier issues are factored in.

Add another hour or two for testing across carriers and tuning the message timing. The return on a single recovered lead still covers the entire setup, but the build itself is real work.
